# Exemplar — selective outcome reporting / outcome switching (trials)

**Finding class:** the manuscript's reported **primary outcome** differs from the one in the
trial registration / protocol — a registered secondary promoted to primary, the registered
primary demoted to secondary or dropped, or its timepoint/metric changed — or outcomes are
added/dropped, without a disclosed, dated amendment.
**Typical severity:** **Major** (integrity-adjacent) when the switch is undisclosed and moves
toward a statistically significant result; **Minor** when the change is a documented,
date-stamped, rationale-backed protocol amendment.

## What the reviewer noticed

The registration (ClinicalTrials.gov / registry ID, given in Methods) lists the primary
outcome as [X at timepoint T], but the manuscript reports [Y] as primary; [X] appears only in
a supplementary table, or not at all. No amendment date or rationale is given, and the
Abstract leads with the [Y] result.

## Weak phrasing (avoid)

> The authors switched their primary outcome. This is outcome reporting bias and is not
> acceptable.

(An accusation with no anchor, no path, and a gatekeeping tone.)

## Strong phrasing (model this)

> Comparing the registration ([registry ID]) with the manuscript, the registered primary
> outcome appears to be [X at timepoint T], while the reported primary is [Y]. Could you
> clarify whether this change was a pre-specified protocol amendment — and if so, add its
> date and rationale (and the protocol version) to the Methods? It would also strengthen the
> paper to **report the registered primary outcome's result regardless of its direction**, so
> readers can compare the pre-specified and reported analyses. CONSORT item 6b asks for any
> changes to outcomes after the trial began, with reasons; stating this explicitly in the
> Methods (outcomes / protocol-amendment reporting) would resolve the concern. If [X] and [Y]
> were both pre-specified,
> noting the primary/secondary hierarchy as registered, with the multiplicity handling, would
> make the result interpretable.

## Severity calibration

If the change is undisclosed and the reported primary is the one that reached significance,
this is **Major** — the analysis a reader trusts may be the one selected after seeing the
data. If the manuscript already discloses a dated, justified amendment and reports the
registered primary regardless, a clearer cross-reference is a reasonable **Minor**.

## Related checks

CONSORT items 6a/6b (outcomes; changes after trial began) and the registered-vs-reported
cross-check; estimand and multiplicity handling for the primary; `/check-reporting` CONSORT,
under which a pre-specified, registration-matched primary outcome is the non-waivable element;
the Confidential Comments to the Editor when an undisclosed switch favours significance.
